Koi/Nishikigoi (brocaded carp)

In North America, koi are used in ponds on golf courses to eat water-born insects.

  • Scientific Name: Cyprinus carpio
  • Average Length: 2 to 3 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 50 to 70 years
  • Habitat: In the wild, koi have been released into nearly every continent except for Antartica.

Koi come in a large variety of colors, mainly white, black, red, yellow, and blue. They can grow to be quite large and quite old depending on how well the fish are taken care of.
